Our new views on ideal jobs

Aidyn:
After viewing the data from Burning Glass my opinion on my ideal job has changed in terms of the scope of what I can pursue after university, I have found that data jobs that use SQL seem to be more in high demand, this helped reinforce my pursuit for a job in the data realm and also highlight that there is many more in demand jobs over data analyst that I should look into before making it my ideal job.

Sam (Mun Kit):
Even after looking at the Burning Glass data my opinion of my ideal job has not changed however I am open to more options as there are more higher ranked jobs in demand which interest me like Java developer which can boost my chances to securing a job as one of the highest demanded skills is in JAVA/ Javascript is needed.

Owen:
Reflecting on the Burning Glass data, I have realised that the job title of a Network Engineer is definitely a lot more difficult to find work than i expected. Whilst it is not explicitly shown on the list, a Systems Engineer which is of similar nature is ranked second last on the list. Working with data is obviously a growing field in the IT industry judging by the fact that SQL skills are ranked #1 in active selections.

Zach:
Looking at the data presented, it appears that the job title i have chosen, the specific skills and general skills are all in high demand, which will provide a high paying job with such skills. If the job title and skills required with it do not change in the coming years, my opinion of my ideal job has not changed.

Harrison:
After looking at the Burning Glass data, I can see that database administrators are relatively low in regards to importance for employers, only ranking 11th place on the top occupations data, and instead a more general network/system administrator might be a more ideal job if I wish to pursue the management of data/systems, seeing as that ranks fourth in the top occupations data provided by Burning Glass.

Teodore:
The Burning Glass data hasn’t changed the opinion of my ideal job as I already knew that being a Creative Director for a game company wouldn’t have the same demand as most IT jobs, due to its unique specialisation. The demand of this job isn’t what attracts me to it, it's the creative outlet and the passion I have for creating something of my own. But I don’t want to solely pursue this passion, I would like to first find some stability with my work by getting into the IT industry for practical and well paying jobs so that I can build up a safety net.
